I am a software engineer at Google Madison, where I lead a team building novel analytical solutions to make data insight discovery more effective and efficient. Our approach moves beyond the low-level nature of classical relational algebra by developing a higher-level query language foundation, which in turn provides a more natural and effective target for automation through generative AI. Our work was highlighted in Future of Data Science, Google Cloud Next 2025. In addition to my core work, I also conduct research in machine learning and trustworthy AI; please refer to my publications for details.

Multi-Relational Algebra (MRA). As a core part of our work at Google, we developed Multi-Relational Algebra, which extends classical relational algebra to support many-to-many transformations over sets of relations. Specifically, MRA extends the data model of the classic relational algebra with two novel data objects: RelationSpace, inspired by the OLAP cube, which models a set of relations that can be identified by a set of dimensions, and SliceRelation, inspired by nested relational model, which models a set of slice tuples, where each slice tuple is keyed by a classic tuple called region, and contains a set of columns, each storing a nested relation. MRA also extends the classic relational algebra with a set of novel operators for manipulating and transforming relation spaces and slice relations. These constructs enable MRA to compactly represent and compute over sets of relations, in a manner that is aligned with modern analysis needs.

Prior to Google, I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, advised by Jeffrey F. Naughton and Somesh Jha. I was awarded a Google PhD Fellowship in 2016.
